Source: Tycho-2 Catalogue (Hog et al. 2000, A&A 355, L27), fetched from
CDS/VizieR (https://cdsarc.cds.unistra.fr/ftp/cats/I/259/, catalogue
I/259) -- 20 raw parts (tyc2.dat.00.gz .. tyc2.dat.19.gz). Same ESA
Hipparcos mission (star-mapper data), freely redistributable astronomical
survey data, standard CDS/VizieR terms (citation of the above paper is
the only real obligation, no restrictive copyright).

bright_stars.npz is a derived, filtered extract: every Tycho-2 entry with
a valid observed RA/DEC (bytes 153-177 of tyc2.dat, always populated,
unlike the optional "mean position" fields) and at least one of
BT/VT magnitude, converted to an approximate Johnson V magnitude via
V ~= VT - 0.090*(BT-VT) (falling back to plain VT, or BT if VT itself is
blank), then cut at V < 11.0, 936897 stars, stored as three float32
arrays (ra_deg, dec_deg, magnitude). Used by camera/mock_camera.py to
render a realistic background star field around the simulated ISS, keyed
to the mount's actual RA/DEC pointing.

Replaces an earlier, shallower Hipparcos-only extract (Vmag < 9.5,
98653 stars): that catalogue is a bright-star-only, ~118000-star all-sky
survey, and was confirmed too sparse for realistic testing near specific
sky positions regardless of how deep its own faint-end cutoff was pushed
-- e.g. only 2-4 stars fall within this project's ~1.83x1.03 deg finder
FOV around Vega or Polaris even at Hipparcos' full depth (checked
directly against the raw catalogue, not just the committed npz's own
9.5 cutoff). Tycho-2 (2.5 million stars total) gives a real, useful
density even at those specific sparse-looking spots (13-74 stars
depending on magnitude cutoff), which matters because polar-alignment
testing specifically points near the celestial pole -- a by-definition
fixed, non-negotiable target, unlike Vega, which was picked and could in
principle be swapped for a denser field.

The V < 11.0 cutoff (not the full V < 15.37 the raw catalogue reaches)
keeps the file a manageable ~9 MB and this mock's per-frame star-field
rendering cost reasonable (measured ~35 ms/frame vs ~21 ms/frame for the
old, much smaller Hipparcos extract, vs ~70 ms/frame for the
uncut V < 15.37 catalogue) while still comfortably covering every
sky position this project's mock needs to simulate.
